About the Collective

A vibrant community of dedicated musicians pursuing excellence in wind performance

In Spring 2025, Brandon Madrid founded the SoCal Music Collective with a bold vision: to create a space where musicians could challenge themselves, grow, and ultimately become the best artists they can be.

What began as an opportunity for Brandon to gain conducting experience quickly evolved into a collaborative community built on passion, mentorship, and a shared love for music. Our debut concert, Legacy, reflected this spirit—honoring the music and leaders who've shaped us while paving the way for new voices to rise.

The SoCal Music Collective brings together handpicked musicians from across California and beyond, each selected for their artistry and dedication. Together, we celebrate the beauty of instrumental music and deepen our connection to its rich traditions through collaboration, passion, and an unwavering pursuit of excellence.

Echoes — 2026 Season

Moments, emotions, and memories that resonate through time and sound

Our 2026 season follows last year's powerful debut of Legacy, a program that explored how music can preserve, honor, and celebrate what we leave behind. This year, we extend that vision with Echoes—a season centered on the idea that certain moments, emotions, and memories don't simply pass… they resonate. They ripple. They echo—through people, through time, and through sound itself.

Saisei Fanfare

Timothy Mahr

A triumphant and heartfelt celebration. Its title, "Saisei," meaning "rebirth" or "resurgence," reminds us that beginnings are never just beginnings—they are echoes of those who came before. Through vibrant brass and soaring textures, the piece pays tribute to the way musical lineage is passed down through generations of educators, mentors, and students, each one continuing the song.

Flying Jewels

Frank Ticheli

A symphonic poem that imagines the connection we all have as living beings. With dazzling colors and breathless motion, it paints the emotional connections between beings—how one spirit can reflect, touch, or resonate with another. In its vibrant fluttering, we hear the heartbeat of empathy, memory, and transformation.

Goodnight Moon

Eric Whitacre • Text by Margaret Wise Brown

A haunting arrangement for soprano and wind ensemble. This setting of the beloved children's book invites us into the echo chamber of childhood itself—a world of lullabies, whispered dreams, and quiet wonder. Through this gentle, dreamlike music, Whitacre reveals how even our earliest memories continue to sing softly within us, no matter how far we grow from them.

Erin Ellysha

Guest Soprano

Erin Ellysha is an accomplished vocalist and passionate performer from Southern California, whose musical experience spans over a decade of dedicated choral work.

While studying at the University of California, Irvine, Erin immersed herself in both the Theatre Department and the Vocal Performance program, graduating in 2024. As former board and Assistant Music Director of UCI's nationally recognized a cappella group, Vermillion Vocalists, her voice was featured on multiple award-winning recordings.

Now based near Denver, Colorado, Erin is pursuing a degree in Music at the University of Colorado, Denver, where she continues to refine her artistry and share her voice with new communities.

Brandon Madrid

Founder & Music Director

Brandon Madrid is a passionate conductor and music educator dedicated to creating transformative musical experiences. In Spring 2025, he founded the SoCal Music Collective with a bold vision: to create a space where musicians could challenge themselves, grow, and ultimately become the best artists they can be.

What began as an opportunity for Brandon to gain conducting experience quickly evolved into a collaborative community built on passion, mentorship, and a shared love for music. The ensemble's debut concert, Legacy, reflected this spirit—honoring the music and leaders who've shaped us while paving the way for new voices to rise.

Through the SoCal Music Collective, Brandon is not only carving out his path as a conductor but building a space where musicians can develop their artistry, collaborate meaningfully, and connect deeply with the rich traditions of wind ensemble performance. His leadership style emphasizes mentorship, artistic growth, and the belief that music has the power to unite and inspire.
